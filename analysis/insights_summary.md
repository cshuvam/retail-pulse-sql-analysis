# Insights Summary: RetailPulse SQL Analysis

A simple overview of the most important findings and suggestions from the 2025 sales data.

---

## Key Takeaways:

### 1. Strong Seasonal Revenue Pattern

Revenue consistently peaked during the end of the year, driven by holiday demand. This suggests that preparing more stock and staff for the final months of the year is beneficial.

### 2. Significant Customer Inactivity

About **15% of customers** (30 out of 200) haven't bought anything all year. A simple campaign, like an email with a discount code, could help turn these people into buyers.

### 3. Inventory Gaps: Unsold Products

Five products had zero sales all year. We should look at why these aren't selling and decide whether to lower the price, change how they are shown, or stop selling them.

### 4. UPI Dominates Payment Methods

Most people prefer using UPI for payments. We should make sure this payment method remains easy and reliable for our customers.

### 5. Repeat Buyers Drive Revenue

A group of about 40 loyal customers makes a lot of the store's money. Starting a reward program for them could keep them coming back.

---

## Recommendations:

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| **Inventory** | Review the 5 unsold products. Consider offering a discount or stop selling them |
| **Marketing** | Send special offers to the 30 customers who haven't ordered yet |
| **Seasonal Planning** | Stock up more for the end-of-year holiday season |
| **Customer Loyalty** | Start a reward program for the best repeat buyers |
| **Payments** | Focus on making UPI payments as smooth as possible |
| **Employees** | Reward the top-performing sales employees |

---

## Limitations

- The dataset was generated to simulate realistic patterns but does not represent actual business data.
- The analysis covers a single calendar year (2025). Hence, multi-year trends are not available.
- External factors (market conditions, competitor activity, macroeconomic events) were not taken into consideration.

---

## Conclusion

The SQL analysis of RetailPulse data showed clear patterns in how customers shop and what products sell best. These results help the business make better decisions about inventory, marketing, and keeping customers happy.
