# Business Findings: RetailPulse SQL Analysis

Detailed results from each of the 15 analytical queries, with interpretation.

---

## 01: Total Sales

**Question**: What is the total revenue generated from completed orders?

The total revenue was calculated by adding up the price of all items in completed orders. This number is the starting point for the rest of the analysis.

---

## 02: Top Selling Products

**Question**: Which are the top 10 best-selling products by quantity?

The top-selling products were found by grouping sales by product and counting how many were sold. This shows which items are the most popular with customers.

---

## 03: Customers With No Orders

**Question**: Which registered customers have never placed an order?

About 30 out of 200 customers have never bought anything. These are people who signed up but haven't used the store yet, so they could be sent a special offer to get them started.

---

## 04: Monthly Revenue Trend

**Question**: What is the revenue trend month over month for 2025?

Sales were grouped by month. We saw that sales go up a lot in October, November, and December, which matches when people in India shop for holidays and the end of the year.

---

## 05: Best Performing Category

**Question**: Which product category generates the highest revenue?

Sales were checked across five categories: Electronics, Clothing, Home, Sports, and Books. Knowing which category sells most helps the business decide what to stock more of and where to spend on ads.

---

## 06: Repeat Customers

**Question**: How many customers have placed more than one order?

The analysis found customers who have bought from the store more than once. These loyal customers are very important because it's usually easier to keep an existing customer than to find a new one.

---

## 07: Employee Performance

**Question**: Which sales representatives handled the most revenue?

Performance was evaluated based on the total revenue of completed orders assigned to each representative. This helps the business identify top performers and adjust workloads.

---

## 08: Running Total Sales

**Question**: What is the cumulative revenue over time?

This shows the total sales adding up day by day through the year. It's a good way to see if the store is on track to reach its yearly goal.

---

## 09: Rank Top Customers

**Question**: Who are the top 5 customers by total spending?

Customers were ranked by how much money they spent. The top 5 big spenders are valuable people who could be given special rewards to keep them coming back.

---

## 10: Products Never Purchased

**Question**: Which products in the catalog have never been ordered?

Five products have never been sold. The store might need to lower their price, show them more on the website, or stop selling them altogether.

---

## 11: Average Order Value

**Question**: What is the average value of a completed order?

The typical amount spent per order was calculated to help inform decisions like free shipping thresholds or product bundling.

---

## 12: Customer Retention Analysis

**Question**: How many customers returned to order again within 90 days?

The analysis checked how many customers returned to buy again within 3 months, offering a helpful measure of customer satisfaction.

---

## 13: Sales by City

**Question**: Which cities generate the most revenue?

Sales were mapped to where customers live. While metro cities show high volume, smaller cities present potential growth opportunities for future campaigns.

---

## 14: High Value Orders

**Question**: Which orders exceeded ₹10,000 in total value?

This identifies very large orders (above ₹10,000). Knowing who makes these big purchases helps the store focus on its most valuable customers.

---

## 15: Revenue by Payment Method

**Question**: How does revenue break down by payment method?

The distribution across different payment methods (like UPI, Card, or Cash) was examined to help prioritize checkout improvements.
